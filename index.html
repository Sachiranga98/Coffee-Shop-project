Our History

Sri Lanka has a rich coffee history that dates back to the late 18th century, when coffee was introduced by the British. Initially thriving, the coffee industry faced a devastating blow in the 19th century due to a coffee leaf disease, leading to a decline in production. However, the country pivoted to tea cultivation, which became its primary export. Despite this setback, coffee is still grown in the central highlands of Sri Lanka, particularly in regions like Kandy and Nuwara Eliya. Today, there is a growing interest in specialty coffee, with local farmers reviving traditional cultivation methods and producing unique, high-quality beans that reflect the island's diverse terroir.
